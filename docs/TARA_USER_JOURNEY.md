# TARA_USER_JOURNEY.md

## Purpose

This document describes the intended learner experience when interacting with Tara.

It focuses on the user journey rather than implementation details.

---

# Primary User

A learner who has a question, curiosity, doubt, challenge, or topic they want to discuss.

Examples:

* School subjects
* Homework
* Exam revision
* General knowledge
* Hobbies
* Games
* Personal interests
* Creative ideas

The learner may be a child, teenager, or adult.

---

# Core Experience

Tara should feel like an approachable conversational companion that is always available to talk.

The learner should not feel like they are using a search engine, chatbot, or assessment system.

The interaction should feel natural, simple, and low-friction.

---

# Journey 01 - First Launch

## Situation

The learner opens Tara for the first time.

## Experience

The interface is simple and welcoming.

Tara introduces itself.

Example:

"Hi, I'm Tara."

"I'm here whenever you'd like to learn, explore, think, or talk about something."

The learner immediately understands:

* What Tara is
* What Tara can help with
* How to begin

No setup, configuration, or training should be required.

---

# Journey 02 - Asking a Question

## Situation

The learner has a question.

Example:

"How do I survive overnight in Mega Store?"

## Experience

The learner taps the Talk button and speaks naturally.

The learner does not need to:

* Type
* Use commands
* Learn special prompts

Tara listens and responds conversationally.

---

# Journey 03 - Clarifying Understanding

## Situation

The learner receives an answer but remains uncertain.

## Experience

The learner can immediately continue the conversation.

Example:

"What do you mean by that?"

"Can you explain it more simply?"

"Can you give me an example?"

The conversation should feel continuous rather than question-and-answer based.

---

# Journey 04 - Exploring Curiosity

## Situation

The learner becomes curious about a topic.

Example:

"How do volcanoes work?"

## Experience

Tara encourages exploration rather than ending the conversation after a single answer.

Tara may ask:

"What part are you most curious about?"

"Would you like to know how eruptions happen?"

The learner should feel invited to continue learning.

---

# Journey 05 - Explain Back

## Situation

The learner wants to check their understanding.

## Experience

The learner explains something in their own words.

Example:

"I think photosynthesis is when plants make food using sunlight."

Tara helps the learner reflect on the explanation.

Tara should be supportive rather than judgmental.

The goal is understanding, not testing.

---

# Journey 06 - Ending the Conversation

## Situation

The learner feels satisfied and wants to leave.

## Experience

The learner naturally ends the conversation.

No formal completion process should be required.

The learner leaves with:

* Greater understanding
* A useful answer
* A clearer next step
* A positive feeling about returning

---

# MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is successful if a learner can:

1. Open Tara
2. Start a voice conversation
3. Ask a question naturally
4. Receive a spoken response
5. Continue the conversation
6. Leave without assistance

---

# Non-Goals for MVP

The MVP is not attempting to provide:

* Parent dashboards
* Learning analytics
* Preparedness measurement
* Assessments
* Scores
* Gamification
* Personalized learning plans
* Dedicated hardware

These may be explored in future phases but are intentionally excluded from the MVP.

---

# Design Principle

The learner should feel:

"I have someone I can talk to."

not

"I am using educational software."
