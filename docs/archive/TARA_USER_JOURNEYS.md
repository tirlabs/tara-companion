# TARA_USER_JOURNEYS.md

## Purpose

This document describes potential real-world situations in which learners may choose to interact with Tara.

The objective is to understand user behavior before designing features, interfaces, or technical architecture.

---

# Journey 01 – Curiosity Moment

## Scenario

A learner encounters something interesting and wants to know more.

### Example

Hardhik sees something unusual in a game.

He becomes curious and wants an explanation.

---

## Current Behavior

* Ask a parent
* Search YouTube
* Search Google
* Ignore the question

---

## Tara Behavior

Learner says:

"Tara, why does this happen?"

Tara:

* Explains
* Asks follow-up questions
* Encourages exploration

---

## Success

The learner continues the conversation because curiosity is maintained.

---

# Journey 02 – Stuck During Gameplay

## Scenario

A learner gets stuck in a game.

### Example

Hardhik is unable to progress in Roblox.

---

## Current Behavior

* Trial and error
* Search YouTube walkthroughs
* Ask friends
* Stop playing

---

## Tara Behavior

Learner says:

"Tara, I'm stuck in this level."

Tara:

* Asks what has happened so far
* Helps the learner reason through the situation
* Suggests possibilities

---

## Success

The learner feels supported rather than immediately given the answer.

# Early Product Insight

The first version of Tara is not centered around curriculum.

It is centered around moments.

Common moments include:

* Curiosity
* Confusion
* Exploration
* Explanation
* Revision
* Sharing

The primary hypothesis is:

If Tara becomes a natural destination during these moments, learners may develop a habit of conversational learning.
