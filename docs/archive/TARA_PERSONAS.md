# TARA_PERSONAS.md

## Purpose

This document defines the initial user personas for Tara.

The goal is not market segmentation.

The goal is to understand who might voluntarily talk to Tara, why they would use it, and what value Tara must provide.

---

# Persona 01 – Curious Explorer

## Profile

Name: Hardhik (Representative Persona)

Age Group:
Primary School

Characteristics:

* Naturally curious
* Enjoys games
* Enjoys experimentation
* Learns through exploration
* Frequently asks questions when interested
* May lose interest quickly if interactions become repetitive

---

## Interests

Examples:

* Roblox
* Games
* Technology
* Science curiosities
* How things work
* Random questions

---

## Typical Situations

### Situation 1

Gets stuck in a game.

Needs:

* Guidance
* Discussion
* Problem-solving support

---

### Situation 2

Encounters something interesting.

Needs:

* Explanation
* Exploration
* Follow-up discussion

---

### Situation 3

Wants to share something exciting.

Needs:

* Someone to listen
* Someone to engage
* Someone to ask questions

---

## Potential Reasons To Use Tara

* Immediate help
* Curiosity exploration
* Discussion
* Problem solving
* Story sharing

---

## Risks

* May prefer YouTube
* May prefer friends
* May lose interest quickly
* May use Tara primarily for entertainment

---

## Success Indicator

Hardhik voluntarily starts conversations with Tara without parental prompting.

---

# Persona 02 – Thoughtful Learner

## Profile

Name: Hita (Representative Persona)

Age Group:
Primary School

Characteristics:

* Thoughtful
* Reflective
* Enjoys stories
* Enjoys creative exploration
* May enjoy conversation more than problem solving

---

## Interests

Examples:

* Stories
* School topics
* Creativity
* Imagination
* Personal interests

---

## Typical Situations

### Situation 1

Needs clarification about a school topic.

Needs:

* Explanation
* Reassurance
* Discussion

---

### Situation 2

Wants to explore an idea.

Needs:

* Conversation
* Reflection
* Encouragement

---

### Situation 3

Wants to explain something she learned.

Needs:

* A listener
* Follow-up questions
* Engagement

---

## Potential Reasons To Use Tara

* Discussion
* Storytelling
* Learning support
* Curiosity
* Reflection

---

## Risks

* May prefer human interaction
* May not initiate conversations frequently
* May lose interest if Tara feels repetitive

---

## Success Indicator

Hita voluntarily returns to Tara for conversation, discussion, or learning support.

---

# Early Product Insight

The first Tara users are not defined by age, grade, or curriculum.

The first Tara users are defined by moments of:

* Curiosity
* Confusion
* Exploration
* Discussion
* Explanation

Tara succeeds when it becomes a natural destination during those moments.
