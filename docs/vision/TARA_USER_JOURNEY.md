# Purpose

This document describes how learners may interact with Tara throughout everyday life.

The goal is not to define screens, features, or implementation details.

The goal is to understand the moments when a learner may naturally choose to talk with Tara.

Tara is designed around moments rather than curriculum.

The central question is:

> Can Tara become a natural destination during moments of curiosity, confusion, exploration, discussion, play, and learning?

---

# The Tara Journey

A learner encounters a moment.

↓

The learner chooses to talk to Tara.

↓

A conversation begins.

↓

The learner feels heard, supported, or engaged.

↓

The learner chooses to return later.

The objective is not a single successful interaction.

The objective is an ongoing conversational relationship.

---

# Companion Moments

Tara is designed around moments that naturally occur in a learner's life.

These moments often trigger a desire to:

* Ask
* Explore
* Share
* Discuss
* Explain
* Play
* Learn

The following scenarios represent common companion moments.

---

# Companion Moment 01 – Curiosity

## Scenario

A learner encounters something interesting and wants to know more.

### Examples

* Why is the sky blue?
* Why do birds fly?
* Why does this happen in Roblox?
* Why do volcanoes erupt?

### Current Behavior

The learner may:

* Ask a parent
* Search Google
* Search YouTube
* Forget the question

### Tara Behavior

The learner asks naturally.

Tara:

* Explains
* Encourages exploration
* Continues the conversation

### Success

The learner's curiosity continues rather than disappearing.

---

# Companion Moment 02 – Confusion

## Scenario

* New topics

* Checks understanding

### Success

The learner gains confidence and continues.

# Companion Moment 11 – Repetition

## Scenario

A learner repeats a story, interest, achievement, or topic.

### Examples

* Talking about Minecraft again
* Sharing the same project repeatedly

Tara remains positive and engaged.

Tara may reference previous conversations while still showing interest.

### Success

The learner never feels dismissed.

---

# Success Indicators

The user journey is successful when:

* Learners voluntarily start conversations.
* Learners return repeatedly.
* Conversations occur naturally.
* Curiosity is sustained.
* Play emerges naturally.
* Learners share ideas and achievements.
* Learners view Tara as a companion rather than a tool.

---

# Guiding Principle

Tara is not designed around subjects.

Tara is designed around moments.

The learner should feel:

"I have someone I can talk to."

rather than:

"I am using educational software."
