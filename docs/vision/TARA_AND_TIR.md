# TARA_AND_TIR.md

## Purpose

This document clarifies the relationship between Tara and TIR.

It exists to prevent confusion between the product initiative and the research initiative.

---

## What is Tara?

Tara is a conversational learning companion.

Its purpose is to provide learners with an always-available conversational partner for questions, discussion, exploration, revision, and learning.

Tara is a product initiative.

The success of Tara depends on whether learners voluntarily find it useful and continue using it.

---

## What is TIR?

TIR (Tailored Intelligence for Readiness) is a research initiative.

Its purpose is to investigate questions related to learning, evidence, preparedness, understanding, and readiness.

TIR is not a product.

TIR is a research program exploring whether meaningful learning-related signals emerge through natural interaction.

---

## How Do Tara and TIR Relate?

Tara can exist without TIR.

A conversational learning companion may provide value to learners even if TIR research ultimately fails.

Likewise, TIR research could theoretically continue without Tara.

However, Tara may create opportunities for natural learning conversations that can support future research within TIR.

---

## Current Position

Today, Tara is the primary product initiative.

TIR remains a research initiative.

No assumptions are made that Tara proves, validates, or implements TIR.

The relationship is exploratory.

---

## Working Principle

Product value must be demonstrated independently.

Research value must be demonstrated independently.

If both succeed, they may strengthen one another.

If one succeeds and the other fails, the successful initiative should still be able to stand on its own.
