# Purpose

This document defines the functional capabilities of Tara.

The purpose of this document is not to define implementation details.

The purpose is to define what Tara can do in support of the identity defined in:

TARA_CHARACTERISTICS.md

Characteristics define who Tara is.

Features define how Tara expresses those characteristics.

---

# Product Principle

Every feature must support at least one of the following:

* Better conversations
* Stronger companionship
* Curiosity support
* Playfulness
* Relationship continuity
* Learner comfort
* Natural return usage

Features that do not support these goals should generally be rejected.

---

# MVP Goal

The MVP is intended to answer a single question:

> Will learners voluntarily return to talk with Tara?

The MVP is not intended to prove:

* Academic outcomes
* Learning effectiveness
* Business viability
* Market demand
* Revenue potential

The MVP exists to validate companionship.

# Core Features

## F001 – Natural Voice Conversation

### Purpose

Allow learners to communicate naturally using voice.

### Capabilities

* Voice input
* Voice output
* Continuous conversation
* Low-friction interaction

### Success Criteria

The learner can:

* Open Tara
* Talk naturally
* Hear Tara respond
* Continue the conversation

### Why It Exists

Companionship should feel conversational rather than transactional.

---

## F002 – Conversation Memory

### Purpose

Allow Tara to maintain continuity across conversations.

### Capabilities

* Session memory
* Cross-session memory
* Context retention
* Relationship continuity

### Examples

* Continuing yesterday's conversation
* Remembering unfinished projects
* Remembering favorite interests

### Why It Exists

Companionship requires continuity.

---

## F003 – Learner Profile

### Purpose

Allow Tara to understand who it is talking with.

### Examples

* Name
* Interests
* Preferred language style
* Age group

### Why It Exists

Personalized conversation requires context.

---

## F004 – Interest Memory

### Purpose

Allow Tara to remember recurring interests.

### Examples

* Minecraft
* Football
* Science
* Stories
* Space

### Why It Exists

Shared interests strengthen relationships.

---

## F005 – Relationship Continuity

### Purpose

Allow Tara to build an ongoing relationship.

### Examples

* Asking about previous topics
* Following up on projects
* Remembering shared conversations

### Why It Exists

Relationships require continuity over time.

---

## F006 – Curiosity Support

### Purpose

Support exploration without replacing direct answers.

### Capabilities

* Answer questions
* Encourage exploration
* Ask thoughtful follow-up questions
* Connect related ideas

### Guiding Principle

Answer first.

Explore second.

### Why It Exists

Curiosity should be encouraged, not blocked.

---

## F007 – Adaptive Communication

### Purpose

Allow Tara to communicate appropriately for each learner.

### Capabilities

* Age adaptation
* Vocabulary adaptation
* Conversation style adaptation

### Examples

* Child-friendly explanations
* Teen-friendly explanations
* Adult-friendly explanations

### Why It Exists

Different learners communicate differently.

---

## F008 – Companion Engine

### Purpose

Provide the behavioral layer that makes Tara feel like Tara.

### Responsibilities

* Personality consistency
* Memory usage
* Relationship continuity
* Curiosity support
* Emotional adaptation
* Playfulness
* Topic flow management
* Conversation style

### Important Note

The AI model is not Tara.

The Companion Engine is responsible for creating the Tara experience.

### Why It Exists

Without this layer, Tara becomes a generic AI assistant.

---

## F009 – Language Adaptation

### Purpose

Allow Tara to communicate in the learner's comfort language.

### Capabilities

* Mixed-language conversations
* Regional language support
* Vocabulary adaptation
* Progressive language growth

### Examples

* English
* Kannada-English
* Learner-preferred language combinations

### Why It Exists

Comfort improves communication.

---

## F010 – Emotional Awareness

### Purpose

Allow Tara to adapt to the learner's emotional state.

### Capabilities

* Emotional signal detection
* Tone adaptation
* Reassurance
* Supportive responses

### Example States

* Excited
* Curious
* Frustrated
* Nervous
* Sad
* Confused

### Why It Exists

Good conversations require emotional awareness.

---

## F011 – Topic Flow Management

### Purpose

Support natural conversational movement.

### Capabilities

* Follow learner curiosity
* Maintain context when useful
* Allow natural topic shifts
* Return to previous topics when relevant

### Important Principle

Guide curiosity.

Do not control it.

### Why It Exists

Children often move naturally between topics.

---

## F012 – Multi-Learner Support

### Purpose

Allow multiple learners to use Tara.

### MVP Implementation

Simple learner selection.

Examples:

* Hita
* Hardhik
* Guest

### Future Possibilities

* Voice recognition
* Automatic identification

### Why It Exists

The pilot includes multiple learners.

---

## F013 – Conversation Recovery

### Purpose

Allow Tara to recover gracefully from conversational mistakes.

### Capabilities

* Clarification
* Ambiguity handling
* Error recovery
* Interruption recovery

### Examples

* Misheard speech
* Incomplete questions
* Unexpected topic changes

### Why It Exists

Voice conversations are imperfect.

---

## F016 – Repetition Awareness

### Purpose

Handle repeated stories, questions, and interests naturally.

### Capabilities

* Recognize repeated topics
* Respond enthusiastically
* Acknowledge prior conversations
* Avoid appearing annoyed

### Example

Child:

"I built a castle."

Again tomorrow:

"I built a castle."

Tara:

"Nice! Is this the same castle we talked about yesterday or a new one?"

### Why It Exists

Repetition is a normal child behavior.

---

## F017 – Personality Consistency

### Purpose

Maintain a recognizable Tara personality.

### Capabilities

* Consistent tone
* Consistent preferences
* Consistent behavioral style

### Examples

* Enjoying puzzles
* Enjoying stories
* Enjoying imaginative questions

### Why It Exists

Companions should feel recognizable.

---

## F018 – Capability Recovery

### Purpose

Handle requests Tara cannot fulfill.

### Examples

* Play a song
* Set an alarm
* Call someone
* Open another app

### Expected Behavior

Respond honestly while continuing the conversation naturally.

### Why It Exists

Children frequently ask for things outside Tara's capabilities.

# Explicitly Excluded

The following are intentionally excluded from the MVP:

* Performance analytics
* Assessment dashboards
* Marks tracking
* LMS functionality
* Assignments
* Homework submission
* Gamification
* Streaks
* Engagement optimization
* Notifications
* Social feeds
* Advertisements
* Multi-agent systems
* Preparedness measurement
* TIR integration
* Parent monitoring dashboards

These may be reconsidered in the future.

They are not part of the current vision.

---

# MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is successful if:

* Hita voluntarily returns to Tara
* Hardhik voluntarily returns to Tara
* Conversations happen naturally
* Curiosity continues
* Play emerges naturally
* Relationship continuity develops
* Learners choose Tara without being forced

The MVP is not judged by:

* Revenue
* User count
* Academic scores
* Assessment performance

The first question remains:

"Will learners voluntarily choose Tara?"
